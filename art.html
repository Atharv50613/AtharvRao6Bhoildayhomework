Introduction

Gond art is a vibrant and colorful folk art form from the Gond tribal community of central India:

Origin

Gond art originated as wall decorations in the homes of the Pardhan Gond people, who lived near Madhya Pradesh. The name comes from the Dravidian word kond, which means "green mountain".

Characteristics

Gond art is known for its use of geometric patterns, bold lines, and bright colors. It often depicts religious and mythological themes, as well as everyday scenes from Gond life.

Materials

Gond artists use natural materials and pigments, such as black, deep red, white, and ochre.

Other aspects

Gond art is part of a larger cultural tradition that also includes folk dances, songs, and performances.

Popularity

Gond art has been introduced to many museums around the world and is also popular in regions like Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha.
